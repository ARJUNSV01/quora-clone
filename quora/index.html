Why are programmers so well paid even if that job is pretty easy (I'm also a programmer, it's easy)?

First of all, programming is not easy. In recent years we've had a surge in people studying programming to make easy money. Those who have a passion for it typically last long in the industry. But those who don't often end up switching careers. Programming can be frustrating, meticous, boring, and it requires constant learning to keep your skills relevant. Programming isn't easy for everyone.

And second of all, jobs aren't paid based on how difficult they are. If they were, migrant farm workers would be millionaires. Jobs are paid based on supply and demand. Programmers are in demand, every industry on the planet is digitizing. But we're in short supply, every industry is digitizing. So we get paid big money, at least for now.
